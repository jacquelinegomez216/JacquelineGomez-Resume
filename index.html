I am currently a third year student at the University of California, Riverside majoring in Education for Social Justice. Over the years I have gained experience with children by working/volunteering in a variety of different settings like after school programs, high school classrooms, tutoring centers, etc. I enjoy working with kids of all ages because this is something that I have been passionate about for several years. I came into UCR with the intention of only teaching math in high school but after several courses in college I began to really focus my interests on the inequalities in education especially for minority and English second language students.

This last winter I observed a teacher and student teacher at a local high school in a math classroom that was majority english second language students and I would go in a couple days of the week in order to observe not only the teachers but the students as well in order to deepen my understanding of what it feels like in the classroom for these students and the educators as well. Being a resource to these students is only the first step of what I want to do in my future career.

Being an intern at a tutoring studio helped me figure out that I enjoy working with kids, especially those who do not have the same resources as other students which is who the tutoring center I worked at served. The tutoring center was a great place for me to work within the system in order to best make changes that will benefit these students.
